Why should I?

Stand up paddle boarding (SUP) offers a fun way to play on the water, with the added benefit of a full-body workout. And, since you stand at full height on your board, it gives you a unique vantage point for viewing what's down under the water and out on the horizon. You can also get the benefit of being on the water without buying an expensive boat, a.k.a Break Out Another Thousand. Unless you have what it takes to own a boat, this alternative will get you on the water without the headache of owning a boat.

I started paddle boarding while I was on a camping trip with a few friends. One of them brought an inflatable paddle board and let me use it. The first time you have to find your sea legs but once you have your balance and start paddling, it's easy to get the hang of. I was obsessed after my first time and wanted to find new bodys of water to explore. I live in Virginia near Washington, DC and you can find me paddling on the Occoquan river, resevoir (fountain head) and so many more places. I woke up on a beautiful sunny day, searched where to paddle board in Virginia, and if it was under 30 miles away from me; I'm game and I drove there. It's serene, peaceful, and cathartic to those who are closet philosophers.
